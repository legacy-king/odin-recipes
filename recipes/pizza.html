Description

Pizza is a savory dish of Italian origin consisting of a usually round, flat base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomatoes, cheese, and often various other ingredients (such as anchovies, mushrooms, olives, vegetables, meat, etc.), which is then baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven.

Pizza is one of the most popular fast foods in the world. It is often served with a variety of toppings, including pepperoni, mushrooms, onions, olives, and bell peppers.

There are many different styles of pizza, including Neapolitan, Chicago deep-dish, and New York-style. Each style has its own unique characteristics and flavors.

Ingredients

  • 1 pizza dough
  • 1 cup pizza sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup sliced pepperoni
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 475°F (245°C).
  2. Roll out the pizza dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness.
  3. Transfer the rolled-out dough to a pizza stone or baking sheet.
  4. Spread the pizza sauce evenly over the dough.
  5. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese over the sauce.
  6. Add the sliced pepperoni on top of the cheese.
  7. Sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese, oregano, garlic powder, and red pepper flakes (if using) over the pizza.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly.
  9. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.
